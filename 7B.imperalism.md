# US Imperialism
- Acquiring territory or gaining control over the politics/economies of other nations
    - **Sphere of Influence**: unequal trading privileges with political undertones
    - **Protectorate**: loss of independence for protection by imperial power
    - **Annexation**: aquiring an area as a part of the imperal power
- Purpose
    - Resources
    - New Markets
    - International Prestige
    - [Social Darwinism](6.gilded_age.md#social-darwinism)
        - Anglo-Saxon Countries destined to conquer

## Public Opinion
- Imperialists
    - <ins>Josiah Strong</ins>: moral responsibility to spread Christianity & western culture
    - <ins>Alfred T. Mahan</ins>: *The Influence of Sea Power upon History*
    - [Theodore Roosevelt](7A.progressive_era.md#progressives): eager to build US power thru global expansion
- Anti-Imperialists (League)
    - [William Jennings Bryan](7A.progressive_era.md#prologue): Rejected imperialism on the grounds that it is opposed to American values
    - [Andrew Carnegie](6.gilded_age.md#trusts-and-monopolies): would often go to president McKinley's office to lobby against colonization
- Popular Press
    - <ins>Joseph Pulitzer</ins>: The New York World
    - <ins>William Randolph Hearst</ins>: New York Journal
    - **Yellow Journalism**: Style of newspaper reporting that emphasized sensationalism over facts

## Territorial Acquisition
- Hawaii
    - Ruled by Queen Liliuokalani
    - American missionaries & sugar planters - influence over the Islands
    - **[McKinley](7A.progressive_era.md#prologue) Tariff** no longer allowed Hawaiian sugar to enter the US duty free, 1890
    - The Queen deposed in 1893 by sugar planters who wants Annexation
    - Annexed, 1898
- Samoa
    - US negotiated a treaty with Samoan leaders for a naval station at Pago Pago
    - US & Germany divided the islands between them in 1899

### Spanish American War
- Cuban revolution
    - In 1895, Cubans began sabotaging plantations to force Spanish withdrawl or encourage US intervention
    - Damaging sugar cane - American investments
    - Pulitzer & Hearst printed misleading accounts of Spanish Cuba concentration camps
        - Alleged abuses of the Cubans by the Spanish
        - "You furnish the pictures, I'll furnish the war"
- USS Maine
    - Exploded while anchored in Havana, Cuba harbor with 260 casualties
    - Spain accused of deliberately blowing up the ship
    - Later concluded to be an accident
    - "Remember the Maine!"
- April 1898, McKinley yielded to public pressure & sent Casus Belli to Congress
    - "Put an end to the barbarities, bloodshed, starvation, and horrible miseries"
- **Teller Amendment**
    - April 20, 1898 Congress passed a joint resolution declaring war
    - Declared US had no intention of taking political control of Cuba
    - Once peace was restored to the island that Cubans would control their own government
- Manila Bay, Philippines
    - Commodore George Dewey, navy fleet
    - US troops allied with Filipino rebels, captured Manila on August 13
    - Filipinos did not gain independence like expected
- Invasion of Cuba
    - American & Cuban forces defeated the larger but poorly led Spanish army
    - Ill-prepared
    - 5000 American Soldiers died of malaria, typhoid, dysentery, with only 500 battle deaths
    - Inadequate rifles & ammo, food, medicines, uniforms, and supplies
    - Rough Riders
        - **Battle of San Juan/Kettle Hill**
        - Brought Teddy Roosevelt to national attention
        - US Navy destroyed Spanish fleet at Santiago Bay & Spain surrenders

### Treaty of Paris, 1898
- US gains from Spain:
    - Guam
    - Puerto Rico
    - Philipines, $20 mil to Spain
    - Cuba
        - Teller Amendment, guaranteed Cuban Independence
        - **Platt Amendment**
            - US troops remain in Cuba
            - Install a naval base at Guantanamo Bay
            - US can intervene in Cuba's affairs if necessary to preserve its independence

## WWI
- Causes
    - In 1914, Germany and Austria-Hungary warred with Britain, France, and Russia
    - Industrialization - competition for resources
    - Militarization
    - Secret Alliances formed to prevent war
    - Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand
- Western Front
    - Involved the most participants and caused the most destruction than any conflict up until that time
    - <ins>New Technologies</ins>
        - Trenches
        - Machine gun
        - Mustard gas
        - Flamesthrowers, tanks, and airplanes
        - German U-Boats
            - **Unrestricted Submarine Warfare**
            - Germany warned that ships entering the British waters will be immediately torpedoed

### US involvement
- First American response: neutrality
- Germany's use of unrestricted submarine warfare outraged the Americans
- US prospered in 1915 by sales of war material to France and Britain
    - US could not ship supplies to Germany because of British blockade
- Incidents of Submarine Warfare
    - *RMS Lusitania* sunk by German U-Boats, 128 Americans dead, 1915
        - Wilson responded "US would hold Germany accountable for 'unlawful acts'"
        - Secretary of State William Jennings Bryan found the message too warlike and resigned
    - *Arabic*, a pledge from Germany that no other ships would be sunk without warning, 1916
    - Sussex, 1916
- Zimmermann Telegram, Jan 1917
    - Sent by Germany to Mexico with the intention of conflict with the US
    - Serves as the final straw for Casus Belli

### Preparing the War
- National Security League
    - Led by Theodore Roosevelt
    - Extend aid to US allies
    - Called for greater defense spending
    - National Defense Act, June 1916
        - Increased regular army to nearly 175000
        - Congress approved construction of 50 warships in one year
- Russia leaves the Allies, 1917
- Becomes the Soviet Union, 1921, after communist led civil war
- Mobilization
    - **Selective Service Act**, 1917
        - Draft required men between 21 and 30 to register with the government
    - African Americans
        - Served in segregated units
        - Hoped that the fight overseas would earn equal rights at home

### WWI Homefront
- **Liberty Bonds**
    - Loans from the public and taxes raised $32 billion in two years
- Wartime agencies
    - **War Industries Board**: set production priorities and centralized control over raw materials and prices
    - **Food Administration**
        - <ins>Herbert Hoover</ins>
        - Enabled domestic rationing for more food abroad
    - **Natioal War Labor Board**
        - William Taft
        - Mediated disputes between workes and employers
        - Union membership increased, wages rose, 8 hour day more common
    - **Committee on Public Information**
        - Established to mobilize public opinion toward WWI
        - George Creel, progressive journalist
        - Millions of pieces of pro-war literature
        - Sent thousands of "four minute men" around the country to deliver pro-war speeches
- WWI ends 9.11.1918

## Post War
- Wilson's 14 Points Plan
    - US should try and shape the future of the world by becoming involved in the affairs of other countries
    - Secure a lasting postwar settlement
    - Freedom of the Seas
    - Self-Determination
    - League of Nations
- The Big Four
    - Woodrow Wilson, USA
    - Georges Clemenceau, France
    - David Lloyd George, Great Britain
    - Vittorio Orlando, Italy

### Treaty of Versailles, 1919
1. Germay must admit full guilt for the war
2. Pay $30 billion over 30 years to Allies
3. Reduce military
    1. No airforce
    2. No U-Boats
    3. Army less than 100000 men
4. Give up all colonies
5. Give up territory to create Poland, Lativia, Lithuania, and Estonia
6. League of Nations established & Germany could not join

### League of Nations
- The most important point out of fourteen by Wilson
- Primary goal - preventing wars through:
    - Collective security
    - Disarmament
    - Setting international disputes through negotiation
- Allies agreed to a **mandate system** former Ottoman possessions would be place under the LoN trusteeship
- Had no military to enforce international decisions
- Battle for Ratification
    - <ins>Henry Cabot Lodge</ins>, Senate and majority leader

| Internationalists | Revisionists | Irreconcilables |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "Wilsonians"<br>Ratify the treaty as is | Ratify with some clarifications | Do not ratify at all |

- The US does not join the League of Nations
    - Defeated twice by the Senate
    - Feared the US would get pulled into another European war

### Legacy
- Europe exhausted and in debt
- Germany devastated and vindictive
    - Not able to make reparations payments
- US reinforced its position as a world power
- New countries, Europe and the Middle East

## Post-WWI America
- Wartime Legislation
    - **Espionage Act**, 1917
        - Allowed the government to ban "seditious" materials from the mail
        - Established harsh punishment for spying, sabotage, or obstruction of the war effort
            - Schenck v. United States, 1919
    - **Sedition Act**, 1918
        - Criticism of the government's war policy often resulted in prison sentence
        - No criticism of the president permitted
        - [Eugene V. Debs](6.gilded_age#american-railway-union): 10 year prison sentence for speaking against the war

 ### Social changes
- Few Americans wanted to continue the progressive reforms of the prewar period
- Viewed calls for change with intense hostility
- **End of the Progressive Era**
- Demobilization
    - Returning veterans take jobs from African Americans and women
    - Production decline
        - Rise in prices
        - End to a government controlled economy
        - Recession
- Conflicts
    - Labor related: US Steel Strike
    - Race related: "Red Summer", Chicago, 40 killed, 500 injured
        - Marcus Garvey
            - Jamaican immigrant
            - Called for black nationalism
            - Blacks should take pride in their heritage and develop their own institutions

### Red Scare
- Americans worried the Communist revolution from the USSR would influence the West
- Attorney General <ins>A. Mitchell Palmer</ins> began to gather information on radicals
    - Palmer Raids
    - Nov 1919-Jan 1920, 6000 arrests, most suspects foreign born
- Trials of <ins>Nicola Sacco</ins> and <ins>Bartolomeo Vanzetti</ins>
    - Tried and found guilty of murdering a paymaster in MA under questionable evidence
- **Growing Socialist Movement**
    - Violence against American businessmen and politicians
